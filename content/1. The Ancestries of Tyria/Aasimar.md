Aasimar are creatures blessed with power granted to them by the gods. Aasimar represent the divine realm, carrying with them a blessing from [[Celestia]], and are typically paragons in the mortal realms. Any sapient creature, from humans to kobolds, can become an Aasimar should they receive a blessing from a sufficiently-powerful god. They are a people of otherworldly visages, with luminous features that reveal the nature of their celestial blessings.

An Aasimar, except for one who has turned away from their god, has a link to some angelic, godly, or otherwise powerful being. That being provides guidance to the Aasimar, far more often than any other devotees of that god can expect. The guidance that an Aasimar receives can be as direct as a telepathic message, or as vague as visions, prophecy, or dreams. Creatures are typically only blessed to be Aasimar thanks to their outstanding devotion to their god, or because that creature is uniquely positioned to benefit that god.
## Fallen Aasimar
Messengers and prophets of the gods, the Aasimar are their hands and mouths -- and are expected to behave as such. An Aasimar who, for any number of reasons, decides to defy the word of their god, becomes a Fallen Aasimar. Fallen Aasimar sever their connection to their god, and are stripped of any powers or blessings they have received. They are then cursed to walk the land, scorned by their god, marked by their betrayal. These marks can be as simple as rusted skin, or eyes filled with soot, or as complex as a series of unconcealable glyphs on their skin detailing their betrayal in full.

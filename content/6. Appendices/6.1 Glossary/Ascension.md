In the mountains high above [[Sarn]], a solitary stone pillar stands about waist-high, for the average human. A stone landing has been carved into the ground, forming geometric patterns around the stone pillar. Ascension was a Wraeclastian tradition involving a lengthy pilgrimage to this stone pillar, culminating in a moment of prayer or meditation for the pilgrim.

It was believed that offering yourself up to the stone pillar dissipated one's essential life force, or soul, into the [[Prime Material Plane]]. Unlike traditional death involving rebirth in [[Alethia]], or exceptional death involving memorialization in [[Celestia]], ascension is believed to keep the pilgrim's life force forever bound to the Material Plane.

No bodies, belongings, or traces of life remain at or around the stone pillar. Evidence of many climbs made up the mountain can be found on the trail upwards, yet no signs of life exist immediately around the stone pillar.
## History
- At the end of the ASCENSION campaign, the party completed the pilgrimage to the stone pillar, and ascended.

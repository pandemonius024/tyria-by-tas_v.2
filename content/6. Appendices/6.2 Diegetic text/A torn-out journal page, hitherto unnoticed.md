In the early stages of the ECLIPSE campaign, [[Qatar]] sits idly by as the others discuss next steps. In his bag, he notices a piece of paper poking out of the journal he found at [[Mor Proxima]], a loose page, just now showing itself.

---

*Qatar, during the brief stint that the party spent planning in the Agency of Time's old HQ, a corner of paper pokes out from your bag. The paper, threatening to fall out of your bag, appears to be coming out of the notebook you all found in Mor Proxima. As you idly fiddle with the page, you realize that the paper was stuck between two pages that were stuck together by water damage.*

*The revealed pages possess no more pertinent information than that you learned upon first reading the notebook; details about the ups and downs of the author's journey into Mor Proxima's mines. The loose piece of paper, however, grabs your attention.*

*The page is a deeply aged, threadbare drawing. While the finer details of the drawing have faded to time, you recognize the dyes on the page at once: charcoal etchings, burnt ochre, and kermes carmine. The sight of the pigments takes you back, to you and your friends getting charcoal dust all over your faces; to your father teaching you how to camouflage yourself with kermes carmine and chewed-up vegetable gum; to Fatima's eyelids, dusted a deep red with crushed ochre, her smiling at you under a charcoal sky.*

*You snap out of your memories. The picture itself is a faded impression of 2 people side by side. Both people appear to be dressed in red, flowing robes, but making out any specific pattern is made impossible by the aging. One person is drawn with long hair, and the other is wearing a purple-ish amulet. Written on the sides of the drawing is a message, reading, "To the end, Loid, together, always."*

# Coincidence? Mysterious Sandstorms Destroy Amsan Search Party
*Sand has become the largest halter on journalistic investigation*

**For years I've thought that the state of Amsa was no more than a children's story whipped up to spur the imagination of children.**

Nearly a decade ago, when the Wanwon family house was still around, I wrote an article entitled, "Vinvoia's Ja'Almawt Could Restore the Glory of Hasha -- if it Wanted to".

It's now 633 and the Ja'Almawt are still whipping around in the sands, eating sand worms and talking around the campfire. It's a quiet life for them, but not for the sands.

The "walking city" of Amsa of childhood story fame has eluded investigators for decades. Not even the Ja'Almawt, or as I like to call them, the Ja'Al-NOT, have discovered the legendary city.

That was until an adventuring party, helmed by the gratuitous Emji-Jae of the Wanwon family, and accompanied by such talent as the traveling bard, "Aux Chord", has mysteriously disappeared in a sandstorm in Vinvoia. Weird, don't you think?

The party were well-equipped to handle anything that the desert could throw at them. Vicious sandworms stood no chance against Emji-Jae's swift daggers, and the song of the illustrious Chord would surely help everyone find their way, in the darkest of nights *and* sandiest of storms.

One night, while executing their search for the mysterious city, the party disappeared, as sand suddenly whisked them away. Surely, the party were just underprepared, caught off guard by an unexpected force of nature, and taken too soon by Pharasma's hand. Or were they?

According to premier Vinovian historian and authority on the songs of the sands, Vinovia's own secluded witch Cassandra Lumen has once stated, "the existence of Amsa is \[...\] quite possibly one of the most intriguing cities of the modern time."

Listen to that. One of the greatest brains to have ever graced Tyria, confirming the existence of that childhood "legend", by directly calling it what it is: a city. But why hasn't anyone been able to confirm its existence?

The answer lies within the Ja'Almawt. These people have been walking around the sands of Vinovia since Hasha got Fireballed, and yet they have been, somehow *unable* to find a giant, mechanical city that walks on 4 gigantic metal legs searching for the *everlasting oasis*. Unbelievable!

What do I make of this? I don't want to border on dispensing any *fake news* like our fellows within the castle proper, all I want to do is ask questions, just like any reasonable citizen should be able to. Questions like:

Who are the Ja'Almawt *really?* Can we believe that they've never found Amsa? How did the party with Emji-Jae and Aux Chord go missing after they've *proven* themselves to be capable in any environment that Tyria could throw them in? *Why haven't we been able to find any of the ancient weapons that the Ja'Almawt are said to have wielded in times of old?*

It just doesn't add up folks. What I'm seeing, when I read these headlines and I put the news together myself, is that the Ja'Almawt aren't letting on *nearly* as much as they should, and that the hidden city of Amsa might be just that -- hidden. 

Who's doing the hiding? You'll have to keep tuned in to tell. This was [[Alec Bones]], your most reliable voice in news: the Info Pundit, publishing monthly, on your street.

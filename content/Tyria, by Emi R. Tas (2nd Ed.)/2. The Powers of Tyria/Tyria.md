Tyria is the first lifeform that graced the universe, whose tears imbued the land with the powers it wields today. 

In the beginning, Tyria rose 3 children from clay, the [[Divinarius]], and was content. She spent her days watching and raising her young, playing with them in the grass and hills, patching their wounds with dirt and water. When their arms fell from their bodies, Tyria would wet their shoulders, and press their arms back into place. When their torsos cracked and crumbled, Tyria would fill in the patches with loose mud and clay, and smooth it out with a wide leaf.

The Divinarius were not immortal as Tyria was, however. As time passed, the Divinarius needed more clay, and water, more mending than could be sustained. One day, Tyria spent all her time merely keeping her Divinarius melded together, to no avail. As she affixed their arms to their shoulders, their feet would crumble from a gust of wind, and as she reformed their toes and soles, their torsos would cave into dry clay pebbles. No matter what she tried, they could not be made whole.

Time stopped, as Tyria knelt at those 3 mounds of clay. She stared into their corpses in silence, her heart stilled, and her mind awash with numbness. Tyria curled up into a ball, and offered prayer into the universe; prayers of hope for a life she could not have. As she lamented, she wept, and 3 tears fell from her cheek, falling into the worlds below.

The [[Tear of Time]] fell through time unto King Anton of the Material, breaking the cycle of bloodshed and destruction that plagued his kingdom,

the [[Tear of Space]] fell through space unto Emi R. Tas of Dweomerheart, whose fascination with its grandeur moved her to chart the stars and the lands,

and the [[Tear of Life]] fell quietly into the oceans of the Luminiferous Aether, where it would remain adrift, until it sprouted life upon the Prime Material.

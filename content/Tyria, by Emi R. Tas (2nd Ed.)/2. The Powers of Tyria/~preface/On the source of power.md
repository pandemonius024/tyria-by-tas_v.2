Gods, deities, abstract powers, and common creatures draw deific power from a common source: the will and emotions of subordinates, worshippers, devout zealots, or other subsidiary creatures. 

A commanding officer might feel their voice amplified for their speech right before a decisive battle, while heroic figures might have their physical limits lifted in times of hopelessness and despair.
## Details
A deities' ability to exert their will upon the planes of [[Tyria]] is directly proportional to the number of worshipping creatures (or plants). Beloved leaders of expansive nations will find themselves able to perform fantastical and impossible tasks to the amusement and wonder of their citizens. While any power granted by the honor and respect of a small village may be little to none, the power granted by the honor and respect of numerous small villages adds up to become significant.

The strength of the deities' power is proportional to the intensity of that worship; the abilities granted by this power are linked to the nature of the worship. For example, creatures whose foundations are tied to recognizable, universal symbols -- such as the Sun, in [[Solaris]]' case -- gain influence over a great many people. For instance, humans give her worship for a bountiful summer harvest; sunflowers dedicate their whole lives to her light; travelers in the desert curse her scorning heat. Since the Sun is such a universal symbol, and its existence so tied to Solaris, Solaris gains the ability to manipulate the Sun's light and heat. 
## The source of power
The source of this power comes from the life within each lifeform, loosely correlated to the creature or plant's weight. A dragon's casual respect is equivalent to a family's fervent devotion. The death of a significant number of worshippers can severely stunt a deities' power, and widespread doubt in a god's strength can similarly diminish their capabilities. 

It is then prudent for those in power to maintain their image, through strict orthodoxy or generous offerings. [[Aris, the unwritten]], has maintained a stable bedrock of supporters by visiting temples constructed in her name, and healing those brought to her.

[[Pharasma, the inevitable]], has maintained her power by monopolizing control over the dead. Pharasma keeps a large number of [[Forgotten]] in [[Alethia]], through deliberately inefficient resurrective practices. Since the Forgotten are washed of their memories before being holed up in Alethia, they are singularly dependent upon Pharasma for any hopes of leaving Alethia, and thus their general hostility and/or penance give her power.

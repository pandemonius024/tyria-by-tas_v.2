Cradeyr is a half-high elf, half-human armorer, who survived experimentation from one [[Cornelius Xandra]]. Cradeyr wears full armor full time, to the point where he does not remember the look of his body, nor his face. The face of Cradeyr's helmet is a skull, an ever-present reminder that, if he doesn't figure out how to cure the ailment Xandra's experimentation gave him, he will die.

Cradeyr was taken by Xandra, and experimented on, as a young child. These genetic experimentation gave him an intelligent mind, but a fragile, corrosive body. Xandra, after learning how badly that set of mutations harmed Cradeyr, discarded Cradeyr. He performed no research towards curing, or even alleviating Cradeyr's suffering. 

Cradeyr fought for a life for himself, developing critical armoring skills to protect his fragile skin from the damaging effects of the sun and wind. Holding fast to hope, he presses on, searching for a way to cure himself. The only salve, the only method that successfully slows Cradeyr's deterioration, is the set of magical armor that he wears at all times.

In the course of searching desperately for any method, any medicine that could fix him, Cradeyr became indebted to one mysterious [[1]], the patron that gave his armor that magically incisive armor. Mere months after Cradeyr donned I's armor, he received a letter in the mail, hailing Cradeyr to Blackwater to repay his debts.

After toiling under I's command in Solva, suddenly, all leads from I went cold. Unsure and undirected, Cradeyr disbanded from the party of SUNDER, and went on his own way, searching for any leads that could cure him of his ailment.

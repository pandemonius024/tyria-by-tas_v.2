Baël Moloch is a [[Lifetouched]] automaton. Baël received the spark of life during the ETERNITY campaign, when [[Dante du Fleur]] baptized Baël in [[Tyria's gift]], turning him from a mindless drone into a joyful apprentice. After Dante left into the [[Void]], Baël fell under [[Pye, the begotten|Pye]]'s wardship. While under Pye's wardship, Baël garnered a love for living creatures, and yearned to walk among them as one of them. Baël made a humanoid form for himself, with which he travels the lands.
## History
Baël, previously known as B-MO, is [[Dante du Fleur]]'s adopted son, and an automaton. B-MO was originally a small battle-bot used in the defense of [[Lex Lutetia]] against invaders and thieves. After ETERNITY bested these bots, Dante decided to keep B-MO's broken-down chassis in his bag. When ETERNITY was caught in a sandstorm, [[Aux Chord]] subsequently arrived to grant them solace in an oasis, and Dante decided to baptize B-MO in the oasis' waters. When B-MO was removed from the waters, he began to beep in response to ETERNITY member's speech, and his monitor showed signs of life. Since that moment, Dante kept B-MO at his side, teaching him the ins and outs of the world with ETERNITY, teaching him basic alchemy, and learned him swordplay from [[Agnar Lightbringer]].

Following the [[Solstice]], Dante left into the [[Void]], leaving B-MO behind. B-MO returned to [[Amsa]], where he fended for himself alongside the budding [[Arachnis]]; [[Nero du Fleur-Donati]] would prove to be too stubborn to stay around. One day, B-MO crossed paths with the creator and owner of Amsa, another dhampir artificer, [[Pye, the begotten|Pye]]. Pye recognized B-MO from Dante's descriptions, and immediately took a liking to him. Pye offered to keep B-MO in his custody, an offer that B-MO accepted.

*"I'm surprised, after 40 years, he still does not know. Continues to play these games, and can't see what's right in front of him."* 
- Baël, on Nero

B-MO spent the better part of 40 years at Pye's side, learning the ins and outs of Amsa, both the machine and the city. The more time B-MO spent on both top and underside, the more B-MO came to develop a love for all things living. Eventually, that love pushed B-MO to change something about himself, to approach them -- to approach their form, to be with them. B-MO crafted himself a humanoid form, and came up with the name, Baël Moloch. Baël entered his newly built form, and hatched a plan to integrate himself into the form more permanently: he would affix himself to Amsa's tallest spire, and absorb lightning from the heavens.

Pye heavily disapproved of the plan, knowing full well that Baël's exoskeleton could not withstand the full force of an Amsan lightning strike.

Baël went anyway. Strapped haphazardly onto the tallest point he could reach, Baël sat inside his exoskeleton, awaiting immersion -- or destruction. As fate would have it when lightning struck Baël, he wasn't obliterated, circuit board from circuit board. Rather, his original form blended into his new form; his limbs responded acutely to his will. He was born anew. 

Baël set off soon thereafter, in search of life and adventure. Shortly after leaving Amsa and stepping foot on its sands, Baël met [[Callian Estera]], who promptly took Baël gambling.

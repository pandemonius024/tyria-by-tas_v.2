The Eternal Empire was a [[Wraeclast]]-spanning empire, whose capital was at [[Sarn]]. The Eternals fashioned for themselves a predominantly human society, ostracizing and rejecting most-all nonhumans who lived on their lands. The Eternals were governed under an absolute monarchy, with individual emperors ruling by might and power alone. The Eternals were wiped out by an event known only as the "Cataclysm", a devastating calamity so destructive, that hardly a building of the Empire remains.

The Eternal Empire's most lasting addition to modern life on Tyria is their calendar: days' names correspond to foundational elements of Eternal culture, while months' names correspond to particularly influential individuals. While most all of the Eternal Empire's legacy has been swept away by the sands of time, analysis of their calendar's naming conventions allows us a glimpse, however brief, into their history.

*Editor's note: the following summaries the author's understanding of the calendar names, and is not to be taken as truth. Little remains of the Eternals to corroborate these interpretations as fact.*
The days of the week (Sat.-Fri.: Solaro, Lunaro, Fiero, Glacio, Galvano, Kaso, and Sacrato) can be divided into two distinct groups: things that the Eternals believed to be naturally fundamental to life, and that which is socially fundamental to life. These groupings are as follows: 
- Solaro and Lunaro refer to Solaris and Lunaris; Fiero, fire; Glacio, water; and Kaso, life itself. 
- Galvano, metalworking, a vital innovation; and Sacrato, rites of grace, a core tenet of Eternal society.

The months (Jan.-Dec.: Eterni, Atziri, Vivici, Lurici, Sagari, Vitali, Azmeri, Divini, Derivi, Phreci, Caspiri, Astrali) correspond either to notable historical figures, or the changing of seasons. The likes of Atziri and Caspiri can be linked directly to emperor Atziri and emperor Caspiro. The likes of Vivici and Vitali refer to the celebration of life and coming of spring, and a time of heat and sun, respectively.

The Vykem, or Wykin while [[Manahaze|Manahazed]], were the once-brainwashed servants of [[Rox]] who eventually liberated themselves from Rox' control. As servants of Rox, they were known to themselves as the branded, and to others as the Wykin. As freed slaves fighting to take back Basti from the "blind man", they were known solely, as Vykem.

During the Dreamwalker campaign, various members of the liberated Vykem came into contact with the Dreamwalkers. The Vykem, aware of Manahaze, and the Dreamwalker's current state, took up different approaches to interacting with the party. Some Vykem, like Mael and Dimo, assumed the Dreamwalkers to be too far gone to bother trying to crack, while some Vykem like Psvedai and Eros were much more patient, and earned the party's trust. No matter the interactions with the party, the Vykem remained steadfast in their opposition to Rox, come hell or high water.

\[The following are direct excerpts from Doan's notes, lightly edited.\]
> To begin conquest of Basti, Rox unleashed virulent magic that tainted the eyes and minds of a nondescript group of adventurers, causing them to cast their identities aside, and do his bidding under the guise of heroically serving the kingdom. Rox named them internally the “Branded,” for they were branded with a malignant curse, giving them powers that they would call “mana.” But they called themselves the Wykin, heroes to the throne, branded with elegant W-emblems on their chests.

> Their "king" would order them onto adventures across Basti, where they would see themselves making influential and beneficial developments in multiple regions. Unbeknownst to them, they had, in reality: enslaved halflings, planted a tyranny of elves in oppression of orcs and natives, and more harrowing endeavors.

> Once, the Wykin were ordered on a mission to bring peace back to the Tymfros Range, when a Wykin member found themselves tumbling down a hill into a patch of strange plants. As they lay, dazed on that enchanted soil, waiting for aid, they began to hallucinate -- believed themselves, to be hallucinating. Their mind ached, and their two lives began to collide. Sitting in that patch of strange plants, the Wykin member had the curse undone, the truth revealed.

> The rest of the Wykin learned of the awakened Wykin's claims, marked him as insane, and forced them into a nearby hut, the home of a blindfolded man. The man looked at the unconscious member and graciously accepted to help, and requested undistracted time. When the rest of the Wykin members came back for their hopefully-no-longer-insane ally, the blindfolded man and their ally were gone.

> After a few more adventures, the disappearance of the Wykin member would cause them to become uneasy. Eventually, all came to investigate the patch of enchanted flowers, and in turn become willingly freed from their curse, only to be cursed with the guilt and pain of their crimes. One, named Boore, was fearful of that patch of strange plants, and ran.

> Destroyed and betrayed, they tore their emblems in half and took on a new name: Vykem. They branded themselves with sins and vowed to take Basti back from the Blind Man. The first sin of Wrath, Melio, decided to work as a double agent, and returned to Basti, finding that Boore had been anointed the new (false) king. Melio pledged that the others had become evil, and that he would serve Boore as his knight.

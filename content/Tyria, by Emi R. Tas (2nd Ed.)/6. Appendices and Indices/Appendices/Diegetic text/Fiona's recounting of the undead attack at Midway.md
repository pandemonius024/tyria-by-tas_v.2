Day 4 on the road to F'mdell, 672 AS.

We buried Ludwig and Felis. Valle hasn't been the same. It all happened so quickly; as I try to recount the events that transpired, I'm left scrambling for details. There was rain, mud, screaming, the dead; the sounds of a fight happening around me.

I awoke with a start: rain beat down on my tent, someone called out to wake us up, and the grunts of a melee sounded. I could hear Elias and Sven muttering panickedly to each other -- something about bandits? -- I dared not peek out. Then I heard them. Felis went first, Ludwig screamed after him, and then stopped. 

I felt frozen, cold to the earth, my head filled to bursting with blood. I was certain I was going to die -- I couldn't bear to sit still any longer, just waiting to die, I left my tent.

The rain wet my hair, the Darkness left scant few things see-able. I peered out into the dark for a moment, until I began to make out a barely-visible form. Two sunken eyes met my gaze, her back hunched like my *oma*. I remember feeling confused, despaired: "what is this thing doing here?", I remember thinking. Then I saw them. Two ghastly skulls peering over her doubled over back, crossbows in hand. The moment I saw them, they adjusted their aims, till I could see nothing but the bolt's head; I should've ducked, moved, or even cried out. But all I could remember was staring into those empty, empty eyes. I was no more than a standing target.

Then they fell. Their heads flew into the darkness behind them, and that was that. The *oma* quickly left behind Valle's tent, and that was all. By the time I had turned the corner, the undead were pacified, and Ludwig and Felis were dead. She had rejoined her fellows.

We left Ludwig's hammer with him. I think I'm still processing what happened. As we walk and I write, I still expect him to be by Valle's side, just as quiet as he always is. Except he isn't.

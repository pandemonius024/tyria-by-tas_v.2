Brudel was a city in northern [[Vinovia]], one ruled by church and blade, and the center of its conquered territories. Its ruler at the end of its lifespan, the warlord [[Tyrus, the warbringer|Tyrus Terim]], maintained his ever-expansive empire by keeping a tight grip on his conquered territory's mobility. The movement of goods between regions was administered by Tyrus' army; the production and creation of varietal mass-produced goods (i.e. lumber, farming) was also handled by conscripts and the indebted. Dissent among the populace was kept under control by that same army. Indoctrination into Tyrus' preferred religion, [[Ka]], was also ensured by the presence of Tyrus' military might.
## History
In 231 BS, settlers that would create Brudel left from [[Hasha]]. After securing a small foothold at the base of the Vardousia Range, the settlement grew outwardly and aggressively in search of new food and water sources. Centuries of growth and expansion ensue.

In 11 AS, under Tyrus Terim's leadership, the Brudeli marched from Brudel onto Hasha. A months-long siege begun.

In 12 AS, the siege on Hasha concluded, resulting in the complete annihilation of Hasha, as well as the annihilation of Brudel's foot soldiers. Few survivors, Tyrus included, managed to escape the devastation; Tyrus decided not to rejoin his people, however.. Leaderless and fractured, Brudel quickly fell into anarchy, splintering the city-state beyond remiss, and ultimately reducing Brudel to uninhabited ruins.

Ipomoea Canyon is a deep canyon, cutting into the earth south of the eastern tip of the [[Vardousia Range]]. Ipomoea Canyon is home to the eponymous [[Ipomoea]]. 

Deep aquifers beneath the desert feed dozens of subterranean rivers, which all eventually converge at the Alba Falls, a series of waterfalls on the northern edge of Ipomoea Canyon. The Alba Falls feed into the Calnyctus River, which carves through the bottom of the canyon, winding its way southwards before disappearing into the caves beneath [[Vinovia]]. The Calnyctus eventually drains into the Loch Vasiliča. A dense jungle grows out of the humid canyon air, covering the canyon's walls and floor. 

From the surface, only the tallest trees betray the presence of this desert oasis. Before the Solstice, Ipomoea was an isolated village occasionally visited by traders that stumbled into its ground-spurting treetops. Following the Solstice, the arid badlands that surrounded the canyon have been replaced by Shadowfallen swamps, infested with abominations.

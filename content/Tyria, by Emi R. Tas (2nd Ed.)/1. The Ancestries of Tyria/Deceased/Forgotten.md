The Forgotten are all souls that fail to be [[Remembered]]. As such, becoming Forgotten is the fate of most all mortal souls. On death, non-remembered mortal souls are captured by the flow of the river Acheron, whereupon they arrive in [[Alethia]], the domain of [[Pharasma, the inevitable]]. They are escorted down the rivers Acheron and Cocytus by images of ancestral selves, who deposit the newly-fallen soul to the river Lethe. The river Lethe, when waded through or drunk from, cleanse the fallen soul of their memories in life, and prepares them for rebirth in the river Phlegethon. Fallen souls who drink or wade through the river Lethe become well and proper, Forgotten.

The Forgotten take on a uniform appearance of sickly blue flames, whose flickering visage are able to express the suggestion of emotions. Forgotten within Alethia are able to communicate in one, common language. While remnants of a Forgotten's personality may persist through the waters of the river Lethe, no memories exist to link the Forgotten to their identity in life. The Forgotten are pulled, ever gently, to the mouth of the river Phlegethon, whose luminous waters wash the Forgotten into the aether, to be recaptured by the mortal realms once more.

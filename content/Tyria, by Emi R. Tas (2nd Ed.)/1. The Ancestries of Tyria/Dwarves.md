Dwarves are a short, stout peoples, with a hearty nature and a propensity for beard-growing. Modern dwarves are a mostly-dead lineage of miners, blacksmiths, armorers, stonemasons, and heavy drinkers. 

Few dwarves remain on or within Tyria's lands. For the dwarves that do remain, however, their impeccable knowledge of earthen composition, and deep skill in smithing and masonry make them invaluable to any sizeable society. Dwarven pride lives on in its few survivors, who leap at any opportunity to showcase their culture's talents.

While most of dwarven society has been eradicated, their robust architecture and artful constructions live on. Underneath the [[Vardousia Range]], a wide network of caves attach to a complex sewage system, which provided suitable entryway for the party of SOULREND. Exploring through this dwarven cave civilization, SOULREND discovered aqueducts, underground quarries, elaborate decorative pillars, and buildings that last, long after the dwarves passed.

Dwarves who survived the genocide moved further and further underground. Slowly, their society reformed. Staying underground- far, underground, became essential. Seeing the sun was a sin none dare commit, spoken of as if nothing but death awaits. Death was identified, religiously, to be from the same source as famine, conquest, and disease. Dwarves shared stories about the evils of feeling the breeze, the destruction it would wrought upon your soul.

Dwarves, then, became a people of strict orthodoxy: You do not disobey your elders; you do not damage another dwarves' work; and you do not mine straight up. Under these tenants, the dwarves dug further and further away from the Prime Material Plane, and deeper and deeper within Tyria.

Within Tyria's gut, from the same space the Shadowfell occupied, the dwarves lodged. When the Solstice struck, the Shadowfell returned to the Material Plane, leaving the dwarves alone with the Dark.
## Notable Dwarves
- [[Tvashtr]], a blacksmith who aided [[Agnar Lightbringer]] in the reforging of his blade and customization of his armor, is a dwarf who carries a deep sense of survivor's guilt with him. 
- Vladimir Ironfist, an axe-wielding communist dwarf, turned to immense amounts of violence as a retributive swing at the world that took his family away.

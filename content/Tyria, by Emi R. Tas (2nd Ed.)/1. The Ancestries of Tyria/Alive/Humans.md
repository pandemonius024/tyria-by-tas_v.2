Humans are a diverse and adaptable people, with wide potential and deep ambitions. This ambition, and the inclination to utilize tools, have led humans to creating the greatest, and most terrible societies of Tyria. Ill-fitted for life crawling about cave systems, and rarely capable of channeling their will into magic, humans instead explore and exploit the world around them, and the strength found in numbers. Humans run the gamut of making all kinds of cities and gatherings, from nomadic tribes like the Ja'Almawt, to elf-hungry war machines like in Dawnguard.

The earliest signs of humans have been found living in the depths of [[Mor Proxima]]. There, cave paintings have been found dating to ~6,200 BS, depicting glorious hunts, battle against evil enemies, and the nascence of worship of the Light. Historical coverage of the ruins of Mor Proxima indicate that early humans held magic-bearing humans in higher regard, depicting these magicians as holding back their enemies, standing on the front lines, lighting the way for their society. Further study into Mor Proxima shows us humans who were capable of making simple structures out of clay, plates and bowls, as well as the habit of using rocks to break other rocks into sharper rocks. Mor Proxima is widely regarded as the origin of human life upon Tyria.

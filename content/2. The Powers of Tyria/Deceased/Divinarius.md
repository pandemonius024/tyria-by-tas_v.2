The Divinarius were [[Tyria]]'s 3 children, molded out of clay at the dawn of time. Their corpses have come to rest atop Tyria's midriff, and now form the landmasses [[Revanon]], [[Basti]], and [[Solva]].
## The Divinarius in life
The 3 Divinarius were named Rah, Baht, and Shah; eldest to youngest. Tyria fashioned them each in her image and made for them a playground of rolling hills, devious adversaries, and amusing creatures. 

Rah was a boisterous, carefree ball of energy, always fiending for a playful head-to-head. His was a life motivated by personal improvement, and of a love of competition. 

Baht was slender, and the most reserved of the Divinarius, having preferred to occupy themselves with Tyria and Tyria's world. Theirs was a world filled with wonder and delight, contented to be sat creating and merely appreciating.

Shah liked to meet all of Rah's challenges head-on, and had a penchant for forming great tales of piracy and heroism from her antics. She was a high-minded and proud type, for certain.

For a time, they were content: theirs was a life of joy, of accidents, and of bandages. Tyria, ever watchful, labored to ensure that any damage that they sustained would be swiftly repaired. Under her watchful eye and careful hand, the 3 Divinarius were emboldened to live as they wished. The 4 were inseparable. Then, they grew older.

The Divinarius' bodies were not immortal, like Tyria's; they could not harbor life forever. No matter Tyria's efforts, no matter Baht's inventions or Rah or Shah's will, the Divinarius crumbled away. Their appendages and limbs went first, and their sentience went last. They died resting on Tyria's lap.
## The Divinarius' Corpses
The Divinarius' corpses are the landmasses upon which most all society in the Material exist. Their clay, still somewhat stained with life gifted to them by Tyria, sprouted the first people to roam the land, the 3 ancestral civilizations: the [[Elves]], the [[Dwarves]], and the [[Humans]].
